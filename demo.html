This is our beta version that calculates equity share for members in a startup or company.

  • They key variables are an individual’s annual compensation, hours worked as well as invested and contributed cash.
  • The logic is that "hours worked" and "hourly rate" translate into individual's equity ownership.
  • Any "invested cash" is multiplied by 4 and gets added to the individual’s equity ownership. Time is easier to contribute than cash, hence the multiplier is being used to account for it.
    An example: $100 of investment x 4 = $400 in equity contribution.
  • "Start Date" is an important date as it is keeping track of how long an individual has been part of the team. One can estimate a probation period for new members, for instance, 6 months of initial probation and then set 2 years as the equity vested period.
